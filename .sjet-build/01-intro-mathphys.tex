\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

This document presents SJET as \textbf{mathematical physics}: a construction
from minimal axioms to four-dimensional gauge theory, gravity, and cosmology,
with quantitative contact to laboratory and astrophysical data. The emphasis is
on \emph{what is mathematically derived}, \emph{what effective truncations are
selected}, and \emph{how outputs compare to the Standard Model (SM),
general relativity (GR), and $\Lambda$CDM} as used in contemporary phenomenology.

quotient $\hat Q$ at $\Mirror^7(\Void)$ yields three fermion families
(Theorem~\ref{thm:observer-fp}). The universe datum is assembled in
Theorem~\ref{thm:universe-summary}.

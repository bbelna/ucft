**Mechanism 1: Capacity Well Formation (ι=2 Trap)**

The capacity functional (the neuro-analogue of Bal_C) has a landscape V(ω1,ω2,ω3) whose shape is determined by the mirror involution and lower-level constraints (homeostasis, predictive accuracy, social mirroring).

**Mechanism 2: Leaky or Costly Collapse (Π_σ=-1 Failure)**

In the healthy case, Π_σ=-1 cleanly projects out the mirror partners (σ=+1 shadow content). The projection is energetically favored and the resulting |ω_ι⟩ is stable.

- Mirror-partner content (the "what the self would look like from the outside if it were worthless/hopeless") repeatedly leaks into the observable sector.
- This is experienced as sticky negative thoughts that feel both "mine" and "not quite chosen."

Rumination is not just "thinking a lot"; it is the observable sector being continuously re-contaminated by the non-observable partner because the mirror involution is still partially active inside the collapsed state.

When ω*_2 is pathologically high, the other two weights are driven toward zero by the global capacity constraint (∑ω_i = 0 in the diagonal, plus normalization).

- ι=1 suppression → loss of raw affective "approach" tone, reduced motivational vector, anhedonia for simple pleasures.

Even when the system manages a partial collapse into ι=1 or ι=3, the global ledger (the "above" Bal_C) is still influenced by the shadow measure dμ_Γ+1. This is the source of the diffuse "everything feels heavy / pointless / exhausting" that is not fully explained by the content of thoughts. ### 4.5.4 Interventions as Operations on the Structure

**Pharmacological (threshold / potential shifts)**:

**Psychotherapy (external mirror operations)**:

- Psychodynamic / relational: The therapist functions as a high-fidelity external mirror that can reflect back content in a way that lets the system re-balance weights without the usual internal distortion.

**Neuromodulation (direct Φ perturbation)**:
- TMS, tDCS: Local perturbation of the order parameter Φ in networks that realize particular ι directions. This is like giving the system a "kick" out of the current minimum so that the natural capacity flow can find a different attractor.

**Behavioral / lifestyle (forced re-weighting)**:
- Behavioral activation: Externally enforces movement along ι=1 and ι=3 directions, increasing the time spent in those cells and thereby shifting the time-averaged ω* even if V is still biased.
- Exercise, social contact, nature, creative work: All act as strong external drivers that pull the lifetime chart into under-weighted slots.

The goal in all cases is the same: restore the ability of the lifetime index Λ(ι,τ) to visit the full three-slot repertoire with weights close to the healthy (1:10:16) balance, and to maintain a clean enough Π_σ=-1 that shadow content does not systematically contaminate the observable sector.

### 4.5.6 Depression as a Recognizable Psychiatric Condition

Beyond isolated mechanisms, depression presents clinically as a coherent syndrome — a recognizable "condition" that psychiatrists diagnose, that patients suffer as a unitary (if variable) state, and that has characteristic onset, course, impairment, and risk profile. **Neuro-SJET Definition of a Major Depressive Episode**

A major depressive episode is a sustained period (typically ≥2 weeks) during which an admitted lifetime chart remains trapped in a high-ω₂, low-ω₁/ω₃ region of slot space, accompanied by:
- Clinically significant functional impairment arising from the restricted repertoire of accessible collapsed states.
- The presence of either (a) pervasive low mood as the dominant observable content or (b) marked anhedonia (inability to collapse into ι=1 or ι=3 states that would normally carry positive valence or absorption).

The episode is "major" when the trap is deep enough and persistent enough that the projector leakage and capacity imbalance produce the full classical symptom cluster and cross a threshold of impairment.

**Mapping of Classical Symptoms**

- **Depressed mood**: Persistent dominance of negative content in the ι=2 slot; the collapsed observable self is repeatedly colored by shadow-partner evaluations.
- **Anhedonia / diminished interest**: Suppression of ι=1 (loss of appetitive tone) and ι=3 (loss of immersive engagement). Nothing "lands" because the weights that would allow direct positive or absorbing collapse are starved.
- **Appetite/weight change, sleep disturbance, psychomotor changes**: Ledger-level and lower-rung effects. The global capacity balance and the "soldering clock" (τ pace) are perturbed by shadow pressure and by the energy cost of maintaining a leaky projector. These are not secondary to mood; they are co-emergent from the same deformed geometry.
- **Fatigue / loss of energy**: Direct signature of shadow-sector environmental pressure (σ=+1) acting on the overall system even while the observable sector is partially isolated.
- **Worthlessness / excessive guilt**: Extreme form of ι=2 mirror-partner leakage. The negative self-model (the σ=+1 partner) is not merely thought about — it is partially re-admitted into the definition of the observable "I."
- **Diminished concentration / indecisiveness**: The lifetime chart has reduced access to ι=3 (rich, high-dimensional processing) and is stuck in a low-dimensional, high-self-referential loop. Working memory and executive functions are filtered through the trap.
- **Suicidal ideation / behavior**: When the trap is deep and the projector sufficiently leaky, paths that terminate the chart (self-destruction) become locally admitted within the negative ι=2 basin. This is not "wanting to die" as a separate desire but the geometry of the well making non-existence appear as the lowest-energy continuation of the current trajectory. Active suicidality often involves further breakdown in the ability to distinguish observable from shadow content.

**Specifiers and Subtypes as Specific Deformations**

- **With melancholic features**: Deepest ι=2 well + near-total ι=1/3 suppression + slowed or rigid soldering clock (psychomotor retardation). The system has almost no access to non-narrative states.
- **With atypical features**: Partial preservation or paradoxical reactivity of ι=3 access (mood brightens with positive events) while ι=2 remains biased. The trap is "leaky" in the other direction.
- **With anxious distress**: Simultaneous high activation in ι=1 (hyper-arousal) mixed with the ι=2 trap — oscillatory or co-dominant trapping rather than pure suppression.
- **With psychotic features**: Severe leakage allowing delusional or hallucinatory content from the shadow sector to achieve full observable status (the projector fails so badly that certain mirror-partner constructions are treated as collapsed facts).
- **Seasonal pattern**: Periodic external modulation (light, social cues) that cyclically deepens or shallows the ι=2 well via lower-level constraints on V(ω).
- **Postpartum / peripartum**: Massive external "mirror" event (birth, role change, hormonal shift) that rapidly deforms the capacity landscape and/or weakens the projector during a period of high plasticity.

**Course and Natural History**

- **Single episode vs recurrent**: A first trap may resolve when external conditions change the potential or when an intervention restores escape gradients. Recurrence occurs when the landscape retains a residual deformation (kindling analogue) or when the person's admitted lifetime charts repeatedly re-enter the same class of stressors.
- **Persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia)**: A chronic, lower-amplitude trap — the chart moves slowly inside a shallow but persistent ι=2-favoring region rather than a deep acute well. The projector leak is milder but constant.
- **Double depression**: Acute deep trap superimposed on a chronic shallow one.
- **Remission and recovery**: Restoration of the ability to visit the full slot repertoire with near-healthy weights and a sufficiently clean projector. Full "recovery" implies that the underlying V(ω) has been reshaped (or external conditions have changed) so that re-trapping is no longer the default attractor.

**Comorbidities as Overlapping Deformations**

- **Anxiety disorders**: Partial or oscillating leaky collapse with elevated ι=1/2 co-activation. The system cannot cleanly sequester threat-related shadow content.
- **Substance use**: External chemical modulation of the potential or the metric, used to temporarily flatten the well or force access to suppressed slots.
- **Personality disorders / chronic interpersonal patterns**: Long-term carving of the capacity landscape by repeated external mirror interactions (invalidating or enmeshing relationships) that bias the admitted trajectories toward particular wells.
- **Medical illness / inflammation**: Global change to the metric K or to the lower-rung constraints that feed into V, lowering the energy barrier into depressive attractors.

**Differential from Normal Sadness**

Normal sadness is a transient, adaptive shift in capacity weights (temporary increase in ι=2 processing of loss, with preserved access to ι=1 and ι=3 when external conditions allow). The projector remains functional; the shadow content is processed without becoming chronically re-admitted. The lifetime chart can still move. Depression begins when the shift becomes self-reinforcing (the well deepens because the content generated inside it further deforms V) and the projector starts to leak systematically.

**Suicidality as a Special Case**

Suicidal states are not a separate "complication" but the point at which the geometry of the trap makes self-termination of the chart an internally consistent continuation. The combination of (deep ι=2 well + high leakage + suppressed ι=1/3) makes non-existence appear as relief from the contaminated observable sector. This is why interventions that restore even partial access to other slots or strengthen the projector can rapidly reduce acute risk, even before the overall mood has lifted.

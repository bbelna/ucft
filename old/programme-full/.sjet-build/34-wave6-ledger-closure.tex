the nine-thread programme satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Functor coverage.}
    Every Class~I--IV physics-map row carries a named Tier~A/B $\mathcal{F}_D$;
    every Class~V row carries $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{emergent}}$,
    $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{ph}}$, or an explicit out-of-scope tag.
  \item \textbf{Catalog partition.}
    Theorem~\ref{thm:thread-9-partition} partitions the $\sim 65$ open questions
    into $\sim 54$ derived, $\sim 11$ phenomenology, and $\sim 8$
    out-of-scope (Table~\ref{tab:thread-9-partition}).
  \item \textbf{Residual honesty.}
    Table~\ref{tab:residual-refinement-ledger} lists all post-wave Tier~C and
    out-of-scope items without introducing \textbf{A9+} or a third primitive.
  \item \textbf{Epistemic invariants.}
    Primitive axiom count $=2$; named conjecture count $=0$;
    Corollary~\ref{cor:semantic-closure} and
    Corollary~\ref{cor:physics-map-closure} stand.

Corollary~\ref{cor:thread-ledger-target} is \textbf{closed}: the thread ledger
programme is complete; remaining work is Tier~C refinement and experimental
contact, not missing derivation routes.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Item~(i) follows from wave-closure corollaries: each thread names $\mathcal{F}_D$
in its certificate (Definitions~\ref{def:thread-1-evaluator},
\ref{def:thread-2-evaluator}, \ref{def:thread-7-evaluator},
\ref{def:thread-9-evaluator}, etc.). Item~(ii) is
Theorem~\ref{thm:thread-9-partition}. Item~(iii) is
Table~\ref{tab:residual-refinement-ledger}, consolidating gaps from
Sections~\ref{sec:thread-1}--\ref{sec:thread-9} and wave remarks.
Item~(iv) is Corollary~\ref{cor:axiomatic-closure} together with the no-new-primitive
checks in every wave section header.
\end{proof}
